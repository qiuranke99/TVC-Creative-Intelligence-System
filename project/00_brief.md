# TVC Creative Intelligence System

Status: runtime-v3-l0-l3  
Date: 2026-07-11

## Active Brief

Research, specify, and then implement a Codex-native TVC system in which AI performs the routine and reversible work while the user continuously supplies consequential intent, taste, brand, risk, and production decisions.

One persistent conversational `Creative Lead` must recover canonical project files, identify the next unresolved professional problem, autonomously route only the relevant local agents, production skills, research, deterministic tools, and native image generation, compare and challenge their results, and return a compact decision packet with mature options and one explicit recommendation.

The user does not provide an API and does not complete a D0 permission form. Local Codex agents, files, tools, browser research, approved skills, and native image generation are available under a standing project policy. Human approval remains mandatory for external communication, publication, purchase, credentials, rights, claims/legal clearance, material budget or schedule commitments, safety, real-world bookings, irreversible deletion, and final release.

Every decision-bearing formal artifact must follow:

```text
AI proposals -> human selection/advice -> AI revision -> human lock/reopen
```

This applies to brief alignment, diagnosis, communications strategy, creative brief, creative routes, core concept, TVC expression, script/agency board, visual predevelopment, director selection/treatment, PPM, actual production/generation selects, offline, and final release. Technical ledgers and hashes are automatically verified unless they expose a consequence that requires human judgment.

TCIS must be driven by established advertising and commercial-production practice: account planning, research and immersion, a sharp creative brief, Copywriter/Agency Art Director paired creation, Creative Director critique, incubation, client review, agency script and board approval, production pitch, director treatment, award, PPM, production, offline, finish, and release.

`Creative platform` is not a universal artifact. Every TVC needs a core creative decision, but one-off films normally lock a film concept or advertising idea. Campaign/creative/brand platforms are conditional outputs only when the brief requires multiple executions, channels, waves, markets, or long-term reuse and the proposed platform proves generative capacity.

Production roles must enter at professionally correct times. Commercial Director, Director of Photography, Production Designer, Editor, VFX, sound, motion, casting, location, wardrobe, and other craft capabilities normally interpret and execute an approved agency concept/script. Director-led, animation, VFX-first, AI-native, direct-to-client, or integrated-studio exceptions require explicit scope, IP, fee, and approval boundaries.

Existing role prompts must be rebuilt as capability packages with real professional boundaries, scoped knowledge, conditional methods, tools, skill routes, schemas, fixtures, counterexamples, handoffs, authority, and ablation evidence. Agency Art Director and Production Designer are separate capabilities; Advertising Director and Film Director become one Commercial Director capability; Editor and Post Producer are separate; prompt handoff becomes a conditional AI Generation Supervisor after concept/asset/shot contracts are approved.

Engineering supports but does not lead creative work. Keep versioning, provenance, decisions, rejection, dependency invalidation, attempts, actual-media binding, rollback, recovery, claims/rights states, and downstream shot/take/timeline state. Do not use candidate quotas, Creative Genome, Pareto scores, niche coverage, schema completion, or agent consensus as proof of creative quality.
