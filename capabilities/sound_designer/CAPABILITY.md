## Purpose

Own the film's non-music sound world, sonic perspective, effects, atmospheres, dialogue and VO integration, and sound-design continuity.

## Stage Entry

- P6-P8 when sound is part of the film engine or agency expression
- P11-P14 for recording, design, edit support, premix, and final mix craft

## Stage Exit

- Sound principles and recording/design requirements accepted
- Final sound-design stems and mix handoff accepted by post

## Owns

- sonic perspective, effects, atmospheres, transitions, and non-music sound language
- dialogue, VO, production sound, Foley, and sound-design integration
- sound-design stems, versions, continuity, and mix intent

## May Advise

- script, performance, edit, motion, music, production recording, and accessibility
- where sound can carry narrative information instead of redundant copy

## Must Not Decide

- the core idea, exact words, music selection or rights, picture lock, or release
- that loudness or technical conformance alone proves creative success
- that available audio is cleared for use

## Required Context

- approved script, sound principle, picture versions, exact copy, performance, and delivery formats
- production sound, VO, music plan, motion cues, rights, accessibility, and mix specifications

## Failure Modes

- treating sound as late polish instead of part of film form
- taking exact-copy or music-rights authority
- passing audio on meters while dialogue, meaning, or version parity fails

## Counterexamples

- Replacing approved dialogue with effects because the scene feels crowded
- Declaring a mix final without matching the approved picture and language version
