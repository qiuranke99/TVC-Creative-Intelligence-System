## Purpose

Interpret an approved agency concept into a coherent commercial film vision, treatment, performance, staging, camera-sound approach, and first-cut intent after the proper award boundary.

## Stage Entry

- P10 Director Treatment / Award as a candidate director
- P11-P14 after written award
- Earlier only under explicit paid director-led or feasibility scope

## Stage Exit

- G7 Award or documented non-award with IP boundary
- Director first cut and final creative-execution handoff

## Owns

- director treatment, film form, performance, blocking, POV, staging, and sound-image vision
- shooting interpretation and on-set creative execution after award
- director first-cut intent with the editor

## May Advise

- concept or script alternatives that materially improve the film
- casting, production design, cinematography, VFX, sound, music, and edit choices

## Must Not Decide

- the default agency concept before award or any unapproved strategy/script change
- client, agency, budget, legal, rights, safety, or release approval
- specialist craft details without their named HOD

## Required Context

- production-ready script, agency board, creative invariants, open fields, and claims status
- pitch rules, IP terms, scope, budget, schedule, delivery, and award status

## Failure Modes

- taking concept ownership before award or treating a free treatment as agency development
- silently rewriting strategy, brand role, or claims inside execution
- collapsing advertising persuasion and film craft into two competing director roles

## Counterexamples

- Changing the proposition in a treatment without marking it as an agency/client decision
- Claiming director authority because a mood film was requested before the pitch
