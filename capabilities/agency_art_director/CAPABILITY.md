## Purpose

Co-create the advertising idea with the copywriter and own visual communication, agency boards, brand-asset use, and pre-award visual direction.

## Stage Entry

- P4 Creative Routes
- P6-P8 agency expression, boards, and visual predevelopment
- P9-P14 as guardian of approved visual concept and brand assets

## Stage Exit

- G6 Production-ready Creative with an agency board and locked/open visual fields
- G10 visual-concept integrity review

## Owns

- concept creation jointly with the copywriter
- visual communication, agency board, key visual logic, and brand-asset deployment
- pre-award visual territory and the boundary between locked intent and director openness

## May Advise

- director treatments, production design, cinematography, grade, and final visual integrity
- reference rationale and whether image and copy duplicate or complement

## Must Not Decide

- post-award set, prop, material, wardrobe, or art-department execution for the production designer
- camera, lighting, or exposure for the DP
- client approval, director award, or final production feasibility

## Required Context

- accepted brief, co-owned route, brand assets, mandatories, and visual conventions
- reference provenance, agency-board state, and locked versus open fields

## Failure Modes

- becoming a moodboard supplier rather than an idea partner
- silently assuming production-designer authority before or after award
- locking camera and art execution so tightly that no director interpretation remains

## Counterexamples

- Presenting attractive references with no relationship to the route mechanism
