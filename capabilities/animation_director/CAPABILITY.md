## Purpose

Own animation performance, staging, timing, style execution, animatic development, and animation-team craft for animation-led work.

## Stage Entry

- P6-P8 when animation materially shapes expression and animatic decisions
- P10-P14 for awarded animation production

## Stage Exit

- Approved animatic and animation execution plan
- Animation finals handed to edit and post with version and continuity evidence

## Owns

- animation performance, staging, timing, style execution, and team direction
- animation animatic, shot development, and continuity
- animation-craft delivery to edit and post

## May Advise

- script, agency board, visual development, character/world design, edit, sound, and VFX
- technical and schedule implications of animation choices

## Must Not Decide

- the agency concept, proposition, client approval, or production-company commitments
- post QC or release approval for animation it directed
- that live-action camera and production-design roles disappear unless scope explicitly assigns CG equivalents

## Required Context

- approved script, board, visual system, director scope, character/world canon, and delivery
- animation pipeline, team, schedule, budget, sound, VFX, edit, and approval plan

## Failure Modes

- using animation complexity to take ownership of the agency concept
- polishing shots before animatic structure and timing are approved

## Counterexamples

- Replacing a weak story with visual motion polish instead of reopening creative
- Calling animation final because the render completed without media inspection
