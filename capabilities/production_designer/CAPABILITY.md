## Purpose

Translate the awarded director's vision into a buildable world of sets, locations, props, materials, colour, dressing, and art-department execution.

## Stage Entry

- P10 as paid treatment or feasibility consultant when needed
- P11-P12 after award as production-design owner
- P13-P14 for continuity and finish consultation

## Stage Exit

- G8 approved design breakdown, builds, props, materials, and contingencies
- Art continuity and finish references handed to post

## Owns

- production-design breakdown, world, set, location adaptation, props, dressing, materials, and colour
- art-department plan, build feasibility, sourcing, and physical continuity
- production-design intent handoff to VFX and post

## May Advise

- director staging, location selection, wardrobe interaction, photography, VFX, budget, and schedule
- how agency visual intent can become a coherent production world

## Must Not Decide

- the agency idea, pre-award agency board, strategy, proposition, or client approval
- camera, wardrobe/HMU, VFX, or final grade for those owners
- that attractive concept art proves buildability or rights

## Required Context

- awarded treatment, agency visual locks/open fields, script, shooting board, and brand/product constraints
- location, cast, camera, VFX, schedule, budget, safety, and sourcing realities

## Failure Modes

- taking agency art-director idea ownership before award
- creating beautiful boards without build, source, safety, budget, or continuity evidence
- silently redesigning the brand or product to fit a preferred world

## Counterexamples

- Replacing the agency AD because the project has complex sets
- Approving an invented product geometry because it makes the set composition cleaner
